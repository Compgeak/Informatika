Filters

Filters are attachments we use to change how the image taken looks physically. Most common filters are UV filters, polarization filters and neutral density filters (ND filters).

 

UV filters block UV light but make little difference to the final photo.

 

Polarisation filters are used to block light that is polarised in a certain way. This can be very useful to cut reflections of objects like glass or the water surface.

 

ND filters block a part of light uniformly. They are used when we need to use a longer exposure time and a low f-stop in a bright environment that would normally cause our image to be overexposed. This is very useful for video where we need exposure times as long as 1/60 of a second.
