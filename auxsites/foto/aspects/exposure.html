Exposure and Brightness

Exposure determines how bright the image is. We can control exposure with aperture, shutter speed, sensor sensitivity and brightness of the object in the real world.

The shorter the exposure time (shutter speed) the less light will be collected, resulting in a darker image. A higher aperture, more precisely a higher T-stop will allow less light in a given amount of time, again resulting in a darker image. A higher sensitivity on the sensor will result in a pixel outputting a higher value for the same amount of light collected on the sensor but will increase the unevenness of the photo, introducing more noise and producing grainier results.

We can use an exposure table to determine how much light our camera will collect. A higher Exposure value will mean a darker photo (higher because it means the object has to be more brightly lit to achieve optimal exposure).
