Resolution & Angular Resolution

The resolution determines how much detail there will be. Having more pixels on a sensor will allow us to get finer detail and information that a lower resolution sensor cannot get.

 

Angular resolution determines the smallest angle where 2 differences are separable and not seen as 1 unit.

 

A 50MPx image with a low angular resolution will mean that a lot of pixels will effectively display the same color and we will not gain any more detail. Such an image can be downscaled and still retain all relevant detail.
